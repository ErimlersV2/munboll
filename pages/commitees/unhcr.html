UNHCR

The United Nations High Commissioner for Refugees is an award winning (one Nobel Peace Prize in 1954, another one in 1981) UN agency mandated to lead and coordinate international action to protect refugees and internally displaced people (IDPs). The main activities include the protection of refugees’ and IDP’s rights, emergency help through humanitarian action and durable assistance by supporting voluntary repatriation, local integration and resettlement. With currently almost 60 million forcibly displaced people worldwide, this UN Agency doubtlessly has a vast responsibility.
